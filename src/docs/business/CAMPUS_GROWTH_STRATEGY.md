## 1. Campus Network Effect Model

StudentOS has a **local network effect** — not global. The unit of network density is the campus, not the country.

```
Critical density threshold:
  Minimum for "everyone is on it" effect: 15% campus penetration
  Current social app benchmarks: Facebook campus launch hit 50%+ within 2 weeks
  StudentOS target: 15% per campus within 60 days of launch

Why local density beats global scale (for now):
  - "My course-mate is on it" > "1 million Nigerians are on it"
  - Campus-specific content (department gossip, exam help) has zero utility on TikTok
  - Local news/events creates FOMO that drives signup
  - Physical encounters reinforce digital relationship
```

### The Campus Flywheel

```
Ambassador joins → posts campus content → classmates join (FOMO)
  → more campus content → school group created → department groups form
    → academic content appears → lecturers join
      → school verifies → school pays SaaS → data improves recommendations
        → better discovery → more campus users → flywheel spins
```

## 2. Campus Expansion Phasing

### Phase 1 — Alpha Campuses (Month 1–3)
**Target:** 3 campuses, 500 users total

Selection criteria (pick campuses with):
- High WhatsApp group penetration (proxy for receptiveness to group platforms)
- Existing creator/influencer culture (TikTok-active students)
- Social science + humanities + tech mix (diverse content types)
- Not in exam period at launch

**Approach:** Founder-led + single ambassador per campus

---

### Phase 2 — Regional Expansion (Month 4–9)
**Target:** 10 campuses, 5,000 users total

**Approach:** Ambassador-led growth with inter-campus referral mechanic

```
Inter-Campus Referral Mechanic:
  - User from UNILAG posts content → user from LASU discovers it in "Discover"
  - "This creator goes to UNILAG. Join to follow them and discover more from your campus"
  - LASU signup → sees "3 people from LASU are already here" → activates FOMO
  - Inter-campus viral loop adds ~0.3 new campuses per existing campus per month
```

### Phase 3 — National Scale (Month 10–18)
**Target:** 30+ campuses, 25,000 users

**Approach:** Self-serve school onboarding portal + paid campus ads

## 3. Ambassador Program Design

### Ambassador Incentive Stack

```
Tier 1 — Campus Rep (Month 1–6):
  - ₦5,000/month platform wallet credit
  - 3× XP multiplier (permanent)
  - "Campus Rep" badge on profile
  - Priority bug reports / feature feedback
  - Target: 100 new activated users in 60 days

Tier 2 — Campus Lead (Month 6+, top performers):
  - ₦15,000/month cash stipend
  - Creator Verified status
  - Revenue sharing: ₦200 per user they refer who stays active D30
  - Target: 500 activated users, 2+ active campus groups
  - Access: monthly product roadmap call

Tier 3 — Regional Ambassador (Year 2):
  - ₦50,000/month
  - Equity consideration (options)
  - Manages 3–5 campus reps
  - Campus partnership deal ownership
```

### Ambassador Recruitment Process

```
Week 1: LinkedIn/Instagram search for campus influencers at target university
        Look for: department reps, student union, popular TikTok accounts
Week 2: DM outreach: "We're launching at your school. Want to be the first?"
Week 3: 30-min video call — platform demo, expectations, compensation
Week 4: Ambassador onboarded, referral code active, first content posted
```

## 4. Department-Level Expansion Strategy

After school-wide adoption starts, go **deep** before going wide.

```
School arrives via ambassador → School-wide group created
  → Identify highest-activity departments (data: which departments cluster together in groups?)
  → Recruit 1 departmental creator per top 5 departments
  → Department-specific group for: exam questions, project help, internship alerts
  → Department group → natural LMS replacement for informal academic help
  → School admin notices → SaaS conversation begins
```

**Department Content Types That Drive Retention:**
- Engineering: past questions, project sharing, internship alerts
- Medical: case studies, clinical rotation tips, study groups
- Business: case competitions, entrepreneurship, investment club
- Law: mock trials, legal aid clinic coordination
- Journalism: campus news, student publications, media projects

## 5. School Verification Program

### Why Schools Would Pay for Verification

```
School gets:
  - Verified school badge (trust signal for students)
  - Official school announcement channel (direct comms to students)
  - Student analytics (engagement, active students, department breakdown — anonymized)
  - Event promotion tools (exam timetables, registration deadlines)
  - Premium moderation (school-specific content review)
  - Integration with existing portals (LMS, student portal)

Platform gets:
  - Institutional data → better personalization
  - Legitimate school branding → student trust → faster adoption
  - Recurring SaaS revenue
  - Referral pipeline for other schools from satisfied school admins
```

### School Sales Motion

```
Stage 1 — Organic (Month 0–9):
  School adopts because students demand it
  School admin notices usage data
  Platform proactively reaches out when campus density > 10%

Stage 2 — Outbound (Month 9–18):
  VP Digital / IT Director outreach
  Demo: "Your students are already here. Here's what you can see"
  Convert to Basic plan ₦50k/month

Stage 3 — Enterprise (Month 18+):
  Custom LMS integration + API licensing
  Full-school deployment with IT team onboarding
  Price: ₦500k–₦2M/year (based on enrollment size)
```

## 6. Campus Network Effects Quantification

```
Network effect value per additional user:
  Local (same campus, same department): HIGH
    → Creates study group content, direct academic value
  Local (same campus, different department): MEDIUM
    → Cross-department social, entertainment, career content
  Cross-campus: LOW (initially)
    → Increases as creator discovery improves
  National creator: VERY HIGH (for any campus)
    → Popular creators drive sign-ups regardless of campus

Metcalfe's Law adjustment for local network:
  Standard: Value ∝ n²
  StudentOS local network: Value ∝ n² per campus cluster
  → Platform needs density within clusters, not just total user count
  → This is why campus ambassador CAC (₦500) >> national ad CAC (₦3,000) in value
```

## 7. Competitive Moats by Campus

| Moat Type | Mechanism | Replication Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| Social graph | Follower relationships built over semesters | Very high — years of relationship data |
| Content archive | 4-year academic content library per campus | Very high — can't be copied |
| Creator earnings | Revenue dependency on platform | High — creators won't abandon their income |
| Institutional trust | School verification deals | Medium — but takes 6–12 months per school |
| Local identity | Campus-specific culture, memes, events | Very high — hyper-local content has no substitute |
